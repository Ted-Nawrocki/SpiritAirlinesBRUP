Case Snapshot
High-level view

Spirit Airlines is in its second Chapter 11 in less than a year. The first restructuring (Nov 2024–Mar 2025) focused on deleveraging and new equity. The current case (filed Aug 29 2025) goes deeper by:

  • Implementing a $475m debtor-in-possession (DIP) credit facility provided by existing bondholders.
  • Rolling up a substantial portion of prepetition senior secured notes into superpriority DIP claims.
  • Restructuring the fleet via a landmark settlement with AerCap and broad lease rejections.
  • Cutting capacity, routes, and headcount, while targeting a return to profit by 2027.

Capital Structure & DIP Overview
Credit lens

The current case is creditor-driven. Existing secured bondholders are now both DIP lenders and key plan stakeholders.

  • DIP credit agreement: up to $475m, with $200m available immediately upon approval and additional delayed-draw tranches.
  • DIP includes a roll-up of a substantial portion of prepetition senior notes into superpriority DIP claims, pushing those obligations to the front of the recovery waterfall.
  • Legacy equity is effectively out-of-the-money; unsecured creditors and non-participating noteholders likely rely on residual value and plan negotiations.

Fleet, Network & AerCap
Operating lens

A large portion of the restructuring value comes from shrinking and reshaping the fleet and exiting unprofitable flying.

  • Settlement with AerCap, the largest lessor: $150m cash to Spirit, rejection of 27 existing leases, cancellation of dozens of future deliveries, and new leases for 30 aircraft from 2027–2029.
  • Broader court approvals allow rejection of additional leases, reducing the active fleet from roughly ~214 to ~114 aircraft over the plan horizon.
  • Capacity cuts: initial 25% reduction in flying starting November 2025, with further reductions planned in 2026 before returning to measured growth.
  • Headcount: significant pilot demotions/furloughs and plans to furlough ~1,800 flight attendants, with wage and benefit concessions negotiated with unions.
